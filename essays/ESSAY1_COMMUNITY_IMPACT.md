# Essay 1 — A Time You Made an Impact in Your Community

**Status:** Draft complete, Luca approved. Ready to sit. Final polish pass before submission.

---

When I was ten, I walked into Märchen Sagen Academy in Decatur and found something school couldn't give me. Reading and writing felt like walls — I'd freeze on tests, struggle with the page — but at Märchen Sagen, I could create. We'd write stories, build sets, perform on camera, edit, and watch the finished video together. The whole process was hands-on and visual. I could show my ideas instead of writing them down. That's what worked for how I think.

Years later, I came back to work there, guiding the next group of kids ages five to twelve through the same craft. And I noticed the same wall I'd hit: kids could imagine ambitious films, but getting from idea to script to actual production felt impossible. They'd get frustrated, stuck between the vision and the doing. One kid during our AI Filmmaking week was struggling with his lines on green screen — he got frustrated, took a break, and worked through it. He pushed through something hard and finished the video. Watching that, I realized the barrier wasn't creativity or courage. It was executive function: holding all the steps, seeing what's next, breaking a big project into pieces that don't feel like drowning.

As I started working with Claude on a tool to help, it clicked: I could build something that does for the next kids what the program did for me — takes the hardest part (planning, sequencing, getting unstuck) and makes it visual, audio-guided, doable. Not replacing the human stuff — the collaboration, the performing, the pushing through. Just clearing the path to it.

That tool shipped at MSA in July. A staff member can run it without me in the room. Kids use it alongside the team. And the full circle closes: the program that shaped me shaped the thing I built for the kids coming next.
