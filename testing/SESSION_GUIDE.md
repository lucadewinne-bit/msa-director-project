# Test Session Guide — Script Builder at MSA

A simple guide for running a live test with **staff + kids** (Luca observing).
Live app: **https://msa-director-project.vercel.app**

> **Your job today, Luca: mostly WATCH.** Let the staff member drive as much as
> possible — that's the real test. Only step in when they're truly stuck.
> Snags are not failure; they're exactly the notes we want.

---

## Before you start (about 5 minutes)
- [ ] Open the live app on the laptop/tablet. Turn the **volume up** (test 🔊 read-aloud).
- [ ] Open the **Help (❓)** once so staff know it's there.
- [ ] Have the **feedback sheet** (FEEDBACK_FORM.md) ready — paper or on a second device.
- [ ] Say the one rule out loud: **"We describe the story, not the kids — no names."**

## Run the session (staff drives, kids decide)
1. **💡 Brainstorm.** A kid pitches an idea. Staff types it in. Staff reads Claude's
   questions **out loud**; kids answer; staff types the answers back. Keep going until
   the story is ready (the "Make step cards" button starts glowing).
2. **📝 Script.** Staff asks for any changes the kids want — as many times as needed —
   then presses **Publish** to lock the story.
3. **🗂️ Step cards.** Staff presses **Step cards**, then checks them together with the
   kids: **keep**, **skip to Stash**, or **fix a word**. Then **Show the kids**.
4. **🎥 Film (optional).** If it fits your studio time, try setting up **one** shot using
   a card's camera/lights/sound tip.

## What to watch for (this is the real data)
- **Where did staff hesitate or look to you for help?** (note the exact moment)
- **Did the kids stay excited, or drift/freeze?** When?
- Was the **reading level / read-aloud** right for these kids?
- Did the **step cards** actually help set up a shot?
- Anything **confusing on screen**, or any **odd/wrong** Claude reply?
- Did anyone try to type a **kid's name**? (did the app remind them?)

## Right after (about 5 minutes)
- [ ] While it's fresh, ask the staff member the questions in **FEEDBACK_FORM.md**.
- [ ] Jot your own observer notes at the bottom of that sheet.

---

**Keep it light.** It's a test, not a performance. If something breaks or confuses,
that's a win — it's the next thing we fix.
